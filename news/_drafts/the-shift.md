## Two-column compare

### Legacy data centres — High CAPEX & OPEX
Built primarily to serve consumer-facing software-as-a-service applications, legacy data centres lock in high CAPEX and OPEX through a focus on:

- Low latency
- High uptime
- CPU-centric architectures

This legacy infrastructure:

- Is typically located close to urban centres — locking in high land costs
- Is often encumbered with high energy costs, in part due to reliance on fossil-fuel-generated power
- Is built with high levels of redundancy in power and communications, with associated low utilisation and high cost

Together, these contribute to a large physical footprint, further locking in CAPEX and OPEX.

### Next-generation data centres — Lower CAPEX & OPEX
Next-generation data centres — e.g. built to train complex AI models — can benefit from significantly lower CAPEX & OPEX by focusing on:

- Raw compute power
- High internal network speed for increased parallelisation
- GPU / TPU / ASIC-centric architectures

They are typically:

- Powered by low-cost, clean energy
- Co-located alongside power generation, e.g. in remote locations
- Equipped with fewer redundant systems, each with higher utilisation

High equipment density and out-of-city locations enable a smaller footprint and dramatically lower CAPEX and OPEX vs legacy data centres.
