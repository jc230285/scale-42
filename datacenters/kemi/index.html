Area overview
Kemi is a town and municipality of Finland. It is located approximately 30 kilometres (19 mi) from the city of Tornio and the Swedish border. The distance to Oulu is 105 kilometres (65 mi) to the south and to Rovaniemi is 117 kilometres (73 mi) to the northeast. It was founded in 1869 by a decree of the Emperor Alexander II of Russia because of its proximity to a deepwater port.

Project description (long)
Identified by Peter Sladey March 2025. Brownfield industrial island with own power infrastructure, potential to draw ~1 GW (with upgrades). Existing tenants and heavy-set industrial buildings. Two points of entry to the island. Flat industrial island in northern Finland.
