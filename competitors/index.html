Internal · Brand & positioning research

Competitor scan.

Twenty-two data centre, AI infrastructure and colocation peers reviewed for layout, branding, and content patterns. Captured to inform what we lean into, and what we deliberately avoid, on scale-42.com.

What we should steal & what we should avoid

Synthesised across all 22 sites — these are the patterns to lift, and the ones to leave on the floor.

Steal

  • Stats above the fold. Greenscale and Sero1 lead with hard numbers (GW, MW, sites, countries). Reads serious immediately.
  • Headline interactive map early on the home page. Polar DC and Stack do this well. Position a small-format map above the team / news.
  • Be obvious. Cologix fails because you can't tell what they do. The headline should literally say "building large-scale AI data centres in the Nordics" — no jargon, no assumptions.
  • Parallax + clean video banner. Polar DC and Greenscale use this well; Stack's intro animation is the gold standard. Avoid auto-play video that can't be skipped (QScale).
  • Capital & JV story is a unique differentiator. None of the 22 mention their capital sources or joint ventures. Scale42 does (GIG) — lean into that.
  • Per-site detail pages with deep specs. Lefdal and Polar DC do this well. Already on our roadmap.
  • Coherent two-colour brand throughout. Lefdal's green/blue palette is consistent across every page — discipline pays off.
  • Team on a sub-page, not the home page. Polar DC moves team to "About". Frees the home page for the message.

Avoid

  • Forced full-screen world map. EdgeConnect's interactive home-page map is too busy. If we put one on the home, keep it small / static.
  • Burgundy & scarlet palettes. K2 looks dated and aggressive — never go red-brown for infrastructure.
  • Uneven gaps between sections. QScale's variable rhythm makes the page feel amateur. Lock the section spacing.
  • Image-slide-in scroll effects where the page barely moves. Global Switch — feels broken, not premium.
  • Hiding everything in sub-pages. Lefdal and Stack do it badly enough that you learn nothing about them on the home page.
  • 10-year-old blocky layouts. Vantage looks tired. Equinix coasts on scale; we can't.
  • Video banner that fights the brand palette. EdgeConnect — banner colours unrelated to the rest of the site.
  • Forms that get the site flagged. Gigafortitude was blocked by Google over a form. Use a clean Formspree integration only — or fall back to a plain email link.
  • "Solutions" as a navigation item without context. Cologix, Global Switch, Aligned — meaningless to a first-time reader.

Translating into our site

Concrete moves that come out of this scan, mapped to where they go on scale-42.com.

To-do informed by competitor scan

  • Move team off the home page into a sub-page (Polar DC pattern). Keeps the home laser-focused on what we do.
  • Add a small static map snapshot directly under the hero on the home page (avoid EdgeConnex's full-screen world map).
  • Reframe the hero to be unambiguous: "Building large-scale AI data centres in the Nordics." No assumptions.
  • Lean into the JV / capital story — none of the 22 competitors do this. Add a short "Backed by" or "Joint venture" callout (GIG) above the partner strip.
  • Lock section rhythm — equal vertical padding on every section (avoid the QScale problem).
  • Tana-driven content pipeline for sites and news — already on the roadmap (PLAN.md) and reinforced by this scan: every peer has a stale "news" section because manual updates rot.
  • Refresh the "differentiators" block to four points + a sustainability anchor (Greenscale pattern).
