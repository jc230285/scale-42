Four levers

How a Scale42 site compounds environmental advantage.

1 · Renewable-dominant grids

Norway and Iceland operate on 95–100% renewable electricity. Finland and Sweden's northern price zones are dominated by hydro and wind. We site where the grid is already clean — no offsets, no PPA gymnastics required to claim it.

2 · Free-air and water cooling

At 64–69° N, ambient air does the cooling for 8–10 months a year. Coastal and fjord-adjacent sites add seawater loops. Result: a fraction of the chiller energy a temperate-climate facility burns.

3 · Heat reuse, designed in

Where district heating, greenhouses or aquaculture are co-located, sites are designed with heat-recovery loops from day one — not bolted on. Several pipeline sites have signed or LOI'd heat off-takers already.

4 · Brownfield and low-impact land

We prioritise industrial brownfield, decommissioned heavy-industry plots and existing grid infrastructure — minimising land-use change and shortening time-to-energise.
